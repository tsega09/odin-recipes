Eggnog Waffles

Eggnog waffles offer a delightful fusion of the classic breakfast comfort of waffles with the rich and spiced flavors of eggnog, making them a perfect seasonal treat. This festive recipe combines traditional waffle ingredients like flour, sugar, and baking powder with the distinctive taste of eggnog, a holiday favorite known for its creamy texture and warm spices. The result is a batch of waffles that boast a subtle nutmeg aroma and a hint of sweetness, creating a delightful breakfast or brunch option that captures the essence of the holiday season.

To prepare these indulgent waffles, you simply mix together the dry ingredients, combine them with a wet mixture of eggnog, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract, and then cook the batter in a preheated waffle iron. The result is a stack of golden-brown waffles with a tender interior and a delightful eggnog-infused flavor. Whether served with a drizzle of maple syrup, a dusting of powdered sugar, or a dollop of whipped cream, eggnog waffles are a festive and delicious way to elevate your breakfast experience, bringing the warmth and joy of the holidays to your table.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    •  
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
    •  
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    •  
  • 1 teaspoon salt
    •  
  • 2 cups eggnog
    •  
  • 2 eggs, beaten
    •  
  • 4 tablespoons butter, melted

Directions

  1. Mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in a bowl. Add eggnog, eggs, and butter; mix well.
    2.  
  2. Preheat a waffle iron according to manufactuer's instruction. Pour 1/4 of the batter into the preheated iron and cook until waffle is crisp and golden, about 5 minutes. Repeat with remaining batter.
